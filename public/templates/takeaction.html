Background

The rule HUD’s proposing says that any tiny house builder certifying her or his tiny house on wheels as an RV will have to clearly display the tiny house is for “recreational use, not [for use] as a primary residence."

This rule will mainly affect tiny house builders, as this disclosure only needs to be displayed inside the tiny house till it’s sold. And it will not outlaw living in tiny house RVs or tiny house communities. HUD determines federal policy about what an RVs proper use is, but local governments ultimately decide whether they’ll permit long-term residence in RVs within their city and county limits.

Still, this rule will no doubt hurt the tiny house movement: It will confuse would-be tinytarians about the legality of living in a tiny home, hurt the business of tiny house builders, and could affect the attitudes of local policymakers. But it also provides us with an opportunity ...

What’s needed most at this moment to liberate the tiny life are federal regulations that recognize tiny houses on wheels as Small Mobile Domiciles appropriate for primary residence.
